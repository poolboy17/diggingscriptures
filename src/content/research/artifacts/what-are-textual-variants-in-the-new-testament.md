## Types of Textual Variants
 
Not all textual variations are created equal, and understanding their types helps us appreciate the magnitude of the textual history. Variants can be classified broadly as unintentional or intentional changes. Let's look deeper.
 
### Unintentional Variants
 
Accidental changes are precisely what they sound likemistakes made without deliberate modification by scribes. These errors include:
 
 -  **Eye Skips**: Where a scribe's eye might skip a line or word.
 -  **Spellings**: Minor differences due to regional spelling variants.
 -  **Homophony**: Confusions between words that sound alike.
 
 
### Intentional Variants
 
Intentional modifications occur when scribes make deliberate choices to alter the text. Reasons include:
 
 -  **Theological Clarifications**: Scribes sometimes altered texts to clarify orthodoxy.
 -  **Harmonizations**: Making parallel passages consistent with one another.
 -  **Language Adjustments**: Adapting the text for linguistic updates over time.

## Notable Manuscripts and Their Variants
 
Different manuscripts present fascinating case studies illustrating the breadth of textual variants.
 
### Codex Sinaiticus
 
One of the most complete manuscripts, Codex Sinaiticus, provides substantial evidence of textual evolution. Believed to be from the 4th century, its extensive marginal notations hint at ongoing interpretations and corrections.
 
### Codex Vaticanus
 
Another key manuscript, the Codex Vaticanus is highly regarded for its age and almost exhaustive preservation. It reveals some variances, mostly minor yet intriguing, for scholars delving into textual comparisons.
 
### The Papyri
 
These manuscriptswritten on papyrus rather than parchmentare among the earliest and offer views on early Christian textual traditions. They often highlight variants found in later versions.
