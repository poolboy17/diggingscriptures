## Discovery Context
 
### Biblical Reference
 
The First Temple Period, approximately spanning the 10th to the 6th centuries BCE, remains a pivotal epoch in biblical history. This era is marked by the construction of the First Temple in Jerusalem, attributed to King Solomon, and plays a central role in the Judaic narrative. The royal seals recently discovered are believed to be related to this period, providing tangible links to biblical figures and events. Their inscriptions often bear names reminiscent of those mentioned in the Hebrew Bible, inviting scholars and the devout alike to reconsider these ancient texts through a material lens.
 
### Historical Background
 
Historically, the First Temple Period was characterized by both prosperity and conflict, with Jerusalem emerging as a political and spiritual hub in the Levant. The geopolitical landscape of the time was defined by the rise and fall of empires, local dynasties, and a fluctuating relationship with neighboring entities such as Egypt and Assyria. This dynamic environment fostered a rich tapestry of cultural and administrative activities, of which the recently unearthed seals provide crucial insights.
 
### Geographic Location
 
These discoveries are concentrated around Jerusalem and its southern outskirts, particularly in areas such as the City of David. The geographic scope of these findings illuminates the extensive reach and influence of Jerusalem's administration during the First Temple Period. This location has been a focal point for archaeological efforts, emphasizing its historical significance as a central node in ancient Judea.
 
### Recent Findings

This robust scientific approach enhances the credibility of the chronological placement and underscores the importance of these seals as genuine historical markers.
 
### Physical Descriptions
 
The seals are typically small, cylindrical or conical in shape, and often feature a loop or handle for attaching to a cord, suggesting their utilitarian purpose. The material and craftsmanship vary, indicating different levels of prestige and purposes, ranging from everyday administration to ceremonial usage. The inscriptions on the seals are often delicate yet intricate, demonstrating the artisans' technical skills and aesthetic sensibilities of the time.
