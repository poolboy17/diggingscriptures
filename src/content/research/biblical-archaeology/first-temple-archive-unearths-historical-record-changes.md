## Archaeological Evidence
 
### Specific Artifacts
 
The artifacts discovered include clay tablets with cuneiform script, papyrus scrolls, and sealed rolled documents. Among these, the inscriptions reveal transactions, religious texts, and administrative records. The variety and depth of these documents suggest a complex and organized archive once existed within the temple precincts.
 
### Dating Methods
 
To ascertain the age of these artifacts, archaeologists have employed various dating methods. Radiocarbon dating, stratigraphic analysis, and other advanced technologies have been used to confirm that these artifacts date back to the era traditionally associated with the First Temple, providing temporal safeguards to align with historical records.
 
### Physical Descriptions
 
The tablets are remarkably preserved, with clear inscriptions still legible. Made primarily of clay, they exhibit a variety of markings, from simple pictographs to more complex text descriptions. The scrolls, though fragile, offer a rare glimpse into the literary culture of the time, further supported by seal impressions that denote ownership and authenticity.
