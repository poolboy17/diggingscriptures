## Discovery Context
 
The intrigue surrounding ancient temple sites has captivated scholars and explorers for generations. These locations, often described in the Bible, serve as focal points for religious and social activity. In the ancient Near East, temples were not merely places of worship but served as the centers of community life, education, and governance. Biblical references to these sites provide valuable clues about their historical and geographical contexts.
 
### Historical Background
 
The history of temple construction and their uses can be traced to different periods in biblical history, from the Tabernacle described in the Book of Exodus to the First and Second Temples in Jerusalem. Each temple site carries its unique architectural and cultural significance, reflecting the evolution of religious beliefs and practices. By reconstructing the chronology and usage of these sites, archaeologists can identify shifts in political power and cultural influence over time.
 
### Geographic Location
 
The geographic placement of temples often had strategic significance. Many were located on elevated ground, symbolizing divine presence and providing visibility and protection. For example, Jerusalem's Temple Mount is a notable ancient site that, despite its historical and modern complexities, remains a key area of study. Archaeological work continues to reveal layers of history buried beneath the modern city, guiding us in our exploration of ancient geographic contexts.

### Specific Artifacts and Dating
 
Artifacts recovered from temple sitesincluding altars, ritual tools, and inscriptionsprovide essential data points for dating and analysis. An excellent example is the discovery of carved ivories at sites associated with the Kingdom of Israel, indicating connections to broader Mesopotamian cultures and confirming biblical accounts of wealth and artistry.
 
### Physical Descriptions
 
Physical remains of temples, from foundational stones to intricate carvings, offer a window into the artistic and architectural achievements of ancient peoples. Detailed studies of temple construction methods and materials contribute to a more comprehensive understanding of how these sacred spaces were conceived and utilized. The dimensions and plans of temples, often aligned with cosmological beliefs, further illuminate the spiritual and intellectual aspirations of their builders.
 
### Expert Interpretations
