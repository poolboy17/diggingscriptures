## Historical Background of Ethiopian Bible

The historical origins of the Ethiopian Bible, known as the Garima Gospels, can be traced back to the early centuries, revealing a profound cultural and religious heritage. The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church considers the Garima Gospels a significant part of its religious tradition, reflecting the unique religious practices and beliefs of the Ethiopian Church. This ancient manuscript, dated between A.D. 330 and 650 through carbon dating, predates the King James Bible and offers insights into the early development of biblical texts.

The Garima Gospels, written in Geez on goatskin, contain the Old Covenant and the Book of Enoch, providing a distinctive compilation of religious texts compared to Western versions of the Bible. The illustrations within the Ethiopian Bible, depicting saints, apostles, and religious scenes in vibrant colors, underscore the artistic and spiritual importance of this manuscript within Ethiopian culture.

Preserved in a monastery in the Tigrai Highlands, the Garima Gospels have withstood various challenges, including attacks and fire damage, emphasizing their enduring significance. The Ethiopian Heritage Fund plays an important role in the conservation and restoration efforts to safeguard this invaluable piece of Ethiopian heritage. The Ethiopian Bible's historical background, intertwined with legends such as the Queen of Sheba, showcases a rich tapestry of faith, culture, and tradition unique to the Ethiopian Orthodox Church.
