## Transition from Law and Covenant to Grace
 
One of the most significant shifts between the Old and New Testament is the transition from a focus on law and covenant to the concept of grace and a new covenant. In the Old Testament, the law and the covenant played central roles in the relationship between God and His people. However, in the New Testament, the emphasis shifts towards grace and the establishment of a new covenant through Jesus Christ. By comparing passages related to law and covenant in both Testaments, we can discern the theological implications of these differences and further explore the transformative power of grace in the life of believers.

By reviewing the differences in the description and depiction of God, we can gain a deeper understanding of the evolving nature of humanity's relationship with the divine.
