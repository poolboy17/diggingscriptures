## Archaeological Evidence
 
### Specific Artifacts Unearthed
 
Among the fascinating discoveries is the Gezer Calendar, an ancient limestone tablet that seemingly chronicles an agricultural cycle. Inscribed in Paleo-Hebrew script, it reflects a year divided into months marking agricultural tasks, such as harvests and sowing. Similarly, the Lachish ostraca, a collection of ancient pottery inscriptions, detail bureaucratic transactions grounded in temporal systems, further illustrating how time was integrated into administrative and daily life.
 
### Dating Methods and Physical Descriptions
 
Radiocarbon dating and stratigraphic analysis have positioned these artifacts firmly within the timeline of the Israelite kingdoms, specifically the 10th century BCE in the case of the Gezer Calendar.
