## Artifact Dating

 Artifact dating methods have significantly evolved over time, allowing researchers to uncover valuable information about ancient inscriptions, artifact classification, and ancient structures. When it comes to understanding human history, these methods play a crucial role. 

 By analyzing artifacts left behind by past civilizations, researchers can gain insights into the societies and cultures of the past. 

 One of the most common methods utilized for dating artifacts is Carbon-14 dating, which relies on the decay of radioactive carbon isotopes in organic materials. 

 Thermoluminescence dating, on the other hand, measures the amount of light emitted from crystalline materials when heated. Dendrochronology uses tree rings to determine the age of wooden artifacts, while Optically Stimulated Luminescence dating measures the amount of light released from quartz grains. Despite these advanced techniques, archaeologists still rely heavily on the study of ancient inscriptions, artifact classification, and ancient structures to unravel the mysteries of the past.
