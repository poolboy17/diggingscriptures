## Discovery of Ancient Jericho Ruins

The excavation of ancient Jericho revealed a significant pile of collapsed reddish mudbricks along the retaining wall, providing crucial insights into the city's historical fall. This archaeological evidence pointed towards a momentous event in Jericho's past. Among the ruins, researchers found remnants of destruction, with evidence of a massive fire about three feet deep. The burnt remains of large storage jars filled with grain indicated a sudden and catastrophic end to the city's existence. The discovery of these charred storage jars highlighted the intensity of the destruction that befell ancient Jericho.

Moreover, the abandoned state of Jericho following the destruction was evident from the excavated ruins. The city lay deserted for an extended period, underscoring the impact of the event that led to its downfall. The archaeological findings at the site painted a vivid picture of a once-thriving city abruptly brought to ruins. The layers of collapsed mudbricks and the remnants of the fire offered tangible proof of the catastrophic event that befell ancient Jericho. Through meticulous excavation and analysis of these ruins, researchers gained valuable insights into the historical accuracy of the city's fall, shedding light on a significant chapter in Jericho's ancient past.

## Evidence of Jericho's Defensive Structures

Excavations at ancient Jericho revealed a complex network of defensive structures, showcasing a sophisticated system designed to protect the city from potential threats. The city's defensive setup included double walls with an upper and lower city arrangement, typical of Bronze Age traditions. The upper city, likely inhabited by the elites, was situated on a mound and had stone retaining walls for added protection. In contrast, the lower city, where commoners lived, was also fortified but with less elaborate structures. An earthen rampart further fortified Jericho's defenses, highlighting the strategic planning that went into safeguarding the city.

## Dating the Destruction of Jericho

Radiocarbon dating from 1995 provided an essential timeframe for the demise of the city, aligning with the biblical narrative of Jericho's fall. This evidence highlights the historical accuracy of the event and provides insight into the ancient city's fate.

- **Abandoned City with Burnt Storage Jars**: Following its destruction, Jericho was left abandoned for an extended period. Excavations revealed burnt large storage jars filled with grain, suggesting a sudden and chaotic end to the city's occupation.
