## Archaeological Evidence
 
Archaeological endeavors root these stories in the real world, supplying physical evidence that supports or reinterprets Biblical narratives. Artifacts and remains retrieved through systematic excavation are the tangible links between scripture and history.
 
### Specific Artifacts
 
Artifacts such as pottery, inscriptions, and structural remnants provide crucial context for the lives of ancient peoples. For instance, the discovery of clay seals in Jerusalem bearing names found in the scriptures fortifies historical claims about the citys governance and daily life during Biblical times.
 
### Dating Methods
 
Accurate dating is paramount in substantiating claims about Biblical connections. Techniques like carbon dating and dendrochronology allow researchers to place artifacts within a precise historical timeline. These methods reveal chronological details that align with or challenge traditional Biblical timelines.
 
### Physical Descriptions
