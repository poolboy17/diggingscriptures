## Discovery Context
 
### Biblical Reference
 
The First Temple, built during the reign of King Solomon and mentioned extensively in Scripture, remains a cornerstone of biblical archaeology. This market's discovery offers a material connection to the historical narratives that have been passed down through generations.
 
### Historical Background
 
Historically, the First Temple period, spanning from approximately 957 BCE until its destruction in 586 BCE, was a time of intense cultural and political development in ancient Israel. It played a pivotal role in the evolution of religious practices and societal structures. The emergence of a marketplace suggests a bustling hub of commerce and interaction, corroborating historical accounts of Jerusalem as a thriving city.

## Archaeological Evidence

Artifacts act as the physical remnants of ancient tales, each piece a clue to the lives once lived. The First Temple Market site has yielded a diverse collection of artifacts that speak volumes.
