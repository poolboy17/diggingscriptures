### Specific Artifacts
 
The artifacts uncovered from the storage complex include a variety of objects pointing to a well-organized society. Among the most significant finds are large clay jars used for storing grains and oils. These jars, often stamped with royal seals or inscriptions, play a critical role in understanding the economic activities of the temple. Additionally, fragments of writing materials and record-keeping tools have also been found, suggesting a high level of literacy and administrative competency.
 
### Dating Methods
 
To accurately date these findings, archaeologists have employed methods such as radiocarbon dating and pottery typology. These techniques provide a chronological framework, allowing researchers to align the artifacts with specific periods of the First Temple era. By analyzing the carbon content of organic materials and comparing pottery styles, experts can create a timeline of activity within the storage complex.
 
### Physical Descriptions
 
The storage complex itself is characterized by a series of interconnected rooms and corridors, designed to house various goods needed to support temple functions. The physical layout reflects an emphasis on efficiency and security. Stone walls, some several meters thick, were constructed to protect the stored commodities from theft and environmental damage. The architecture reveals a blend of practicality and access to resources, underscoring the temple's role as both a spiritual and economic powerhouse.
 
### Expert Interpretations
