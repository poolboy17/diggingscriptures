## Major Archaeological Discoveries

### The Dead Sea Scrolls

For centuries, scholars have relied on the **Dead Sea Scrolls** as a crucial source for studying ancient Biblical texts. Discovered in caves near the Dead Sea in the mid-20th century, these scrolls contain some of the oldest known copies of the Hebrew Bible, shedding light on the transmission and authenticity of the Scriptures.

### The Pool of Siloam and Hezekiah's Tunnel

Major excavations in Jerusalem have unearthed the **Pool of Siloam** and **Hezekiah's Tunnel**, two significant sites mentioned in the Bible. The Pool of Siloam, where Jesus healed a blind man, and Hezekiah's Tunnel, a remarkable engineering feat built during King Hezekiah's reign, provide tangible evidence of biblical accounts.

**Siloam** The discovery of the Pool of Siloam and Hezekiah's Tunnel not only confirms the historical accuracy of the Bible but also showcases the inventive capabilities of ancient civilizations. The Pool of Siloam served as a vital water source for Jerusalem, while Hezekiah's Tunnel underscored the resourcefulness and engineering prowess of the Israelites in antiquity.
